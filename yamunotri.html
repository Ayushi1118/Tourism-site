Yamunotri Dham

History & Mythology

Yamunotri is the traditional first stop of the Char Dham Yatra and the sacred origin of the Yamuna River. Dedicated to Goddess Yamuna, daughter of the Sun God Surya and twin sister of Yama, it is believed that bathing in her holy waters washes away sins and protects against untimely death. The present temple was built in the 19th century by Maharani Guleria of Jaipur. A major attraction here is the Surya Kund, a natural hot spring where pilgrims cook rice and potatoes as a sacred offering to the deity.

Travelling Way

The journey to Yamunotri combines road travel with a scenic mountain trek:

  • Road: Reach the base camp at Janki Chatti via Barkot from Rishikesh or Dehradun.
  • Trek: A mandatory 6 km uphill trek from Janki Chatti through pine and deodar forests.
  • Options: Ponies, palkis (palanquins), and dolis are available for those unable to walk.
  • Railway: Nearest major station is Dehradun, approximately 170 km away.

Winter Worship

During the winter months when the temple is inaccessible due to snow, the idol of Goddess Yamuna is ceremonially moved to Kharsali Village, which serves as her winter seat.
