Tungnath Temple

3,680m · The World's Highest Shiva Shrine

History & Mythology

Tungnath is the third of the sacred Panch Kedar temples. According to the Mahabharata, the Pandavas built this temple to appease Lord Shiva after the Kurukshetra war. Shiva, avoiding them, took the form of a bull and disappeared into the earth; his arms (bahu) appeared at Tungnath and are worshipped here in the form of a sacred lingam. The temple is believed to be over 1,000 years old and was established by Adi Shankaracharya.

How to Travel There

Tungnath is located in the Rudraprayag district. Travelers usually reach the base via road:

  • Base: Chopta, often called the "Mini Switzerland of India".
  • Trek: A moderately easy 4 km stone-paved trek from Chopta.
  • Road: Chopta is well-connected to Rishikesh (210 km) and Rudraprayag.

Quick Facts

Altitude: 3,680 m

Best Time: May to October (Summer & Autumn)

Region: Garhwal Himalayas

Winter Places Nearby

As the temple closes during winter due to heavy snow, visitors can explore:

  • Chopta: Famous for winter snow trekking and camping.
  • Deoria Tal: A serene lake 12 km from Chopta offering winter Himalayan views.
  • Makkumath: The winter seat where the deity is worshipped during cold months.
