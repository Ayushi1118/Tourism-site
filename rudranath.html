Rudranath Temple

3,600m · Where Shiva's Face is Worshipped · Panch Kedar

History & Mythology

Rudranath is the fourth of the sacred Panch Kedar temples. Here, the face (mukha) of Lord Shiva is worshipped in a natural stone-temple cave. Legend says the Pandavas built this shrine after the Kurukshetra war while seeking forgiveness from Shiva. The temple is set amid alpine meadows and jagged peaks, requiring one of the most rigorous treks in the Himalayas.

How to Travel There

  • Base: Sagar Village, located near Gopeshwar.
  • Trek: A challenging 20 km trek through steep forests and high-altitude meadows (bugiyals).
  • Access: The nearest road head is at Sagar, which is well-connected to Rishikesh.

Quick Facts

Altitude: 3,600 m

Best Time: June to September

Significance: Worship of Shiva's face

Winter Places Nearby

  • Gopeshwar Town: Home to the winter seat, Gopinath Temple.
  • Anusuya Devi: A scenic 6 km trek from Mandal village nearby.
