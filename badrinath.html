History & Mythology

Badrinath is the final and most sacred stop of the yatra, dedicated to Lord Vishnu in his form as Badrinarayan. Located on the banks of the Alaknanda River, it is one of the 108 Divya Desams. According to legend, Lord Vishnu meditated here under a badri tree while Goddess Lakshmi shielded him from harsh weather in the form of that tree. The temple's striking colorful facade stands against the magnificent backdrop of the Neelkantha peak.

Travelling Way

  • Road: Directly accessible by road from Joshimath, approximately 46 km away.
  • Railway: The nearest major station is Rishikesh (~295 km).
  • Air: Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun is the closest airport.
