Kedarnath Dham

History & Mythology

Kedarnath is one of the 12 sacred Jyotirlingas of India. According to the Mahabharata, the Pandavas sought Lord Shiva here to seek forgiveness for the Kurukshetra war. Shiva, wishing to avoid them, took the form of a bull and dived into the earth, leaving his hump as the lingam. The ancient stone temple, believed to be over 1,000 years old, miraculously survived the catastrophic 2013 floods, which devotees regard as a divine act.

Travelling Way

Reaching the shrine requires a journey through rugged Himalayan terrain:

  • Trek: A 16–18 km trek from the base at Gaurikund.
  • Helicopter: Services are available from Phata, Guptkashi, or Sirsi.
  • Railway: The nearest major station is Rishikesh, approximately 216 km away.

Winter Worship

When snow closes the temple, the deity is moved to the Omkareshwar Temple in Ukhimath. Nearby, the meadows of Chopta offer stunning winter views.
