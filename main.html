About Uttarakhand & Char Dham

Uttarakhand, known as Devbhoomi, is nested in the lap of the Himalayas. It is the birthplace of the holy rivers Ganga and Yamuna. The Char Dham circuit—Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath—represents the pinnacle of Hindu pilgrimage, traditionally visited in a clockwise direction.

The journey spans high altitudes (3,000m+), crossing lush meadows, rugged terrains, and ancient forests. It isn't just a physical trip, but a spiritual cleansing believed to wash away sins and grant Moksha.

Traveler's Guide

6 Essential Tips

1. Registration

Mandatory biometric registration is required online via the Official Yatra Portal .

2. Health Check

Acclimatize for 24 hours at lower altitudes like Rishikesh to avoid mountain sickness.

3. Gear Up

Pack heavy woolens, even in summer. Weather changes in minutes.

4. Cash is King

ATMs are scarce and network is spotty. Carry sufficient cash.

5. Local Customs

Respect temple decorum. Photography is strictly prohibited inside sanctums.

6. Environment

Uttarakhand is a plastic-free zone. Do not litter in the mountains.

Before travelling, don't forget to register on the Official Yatra Portal
