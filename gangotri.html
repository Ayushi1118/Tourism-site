Famous For Goddess Ganga

History & Mythology

Gangotri is the second stop on the yatra and the mythological origin of the River Ganga, the most sacred river in Hinduism. Legend says King Bhagirath performed intense penance here to bring the celestial Ganga down to Earth to liberate his ancestors. To soften her descent, Lord Shiva received her in his matted locks. The white granite temple was originally built by the Gorkha General Amar Singh Thapa in the 18th century and later renovated. The actual physical source at Gaumukh glacier is a rewarding 18 km trek further up from the shrine.

Travelling Way

Reaching the shrine involves a scenic journey through the Uttarkashi district:

  • By Road: Connected by a beautiful 100 km route from Uttarkashi town.
  • Railway: The nearest major station is Rishikesh, approximately 230 km away.
  • Trek: While the temple is road-accessible, an optional 18 km trek leads to the physical source at Gaumukh.

Winter Worship

When winter snow closes the high-altitude shrine, the deity is ceremonially moved to Mukhba Village, located 30 km downstream. Nearby Harsil is also a famous winter destination known for its apple orchards.
