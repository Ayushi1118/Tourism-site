Kartik Swami Temple

3,050m · The Highest Shrine of Lord Kartikeya · Kronch Parvat

History & Mythology

Perched atop the Kronch Parvat, Kartik Swami is dedicated to **Lord Kartikeya**, the elder son of Lord Shiva. According to Hindu mythology, Lord Shiva challenged his sons Ganesha and Kartikeya to circle the universe. While Kartikeya set off on his peacock, Ganesha simply circled his parents, declaring them his universe. When Ganesha was declared the winner, Kartikeya, in deep devotion and renunciation, offered his bones to his father. It is believed that his bones are still worshipped in the sanctum of this temple.

How to Travel There

The temple is a hidden gem in the Rudraprayag district, offering 360-degree views of the Himalayas.

  • Base Village: Kanakchauri, which is about 40 km from Rudraprayag.
  • Trek: An easy to moderate 3 km uphill trek from Kanakchauri through dense rhododendron and oak forests.
  • Road: Accessible via the Rishikesh-Rudraprayag-Pokhri road.

Quick Facts

Deity: Lord Kartikeya (Murugan)

Altitude: 3,050 m (10,007 ft)

Best Time: March to June (Rhododendrons) and Sept to Nov (Clear Skies).

Nearby Attractions

  • Kanakchauri Village: The peaceful base village with sunset viewpoints.
  • Rudraprayag: The holy confluence of Alaknanda and Mandakini rivers.
  • Chopta: Located about 70 km away, known as the "Mini Switzerland".
