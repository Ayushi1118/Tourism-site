Dhari Devi Temple

Guardian Deity of the Char Dham · Srinagar, Garhwal

History & Mythology

Dhari Devi Temple is situated on the banks of the Alaknanda River between Srinagar and Rudraprayag. The temple houses the upper half of an idol of the Goddess Dhari, while the lower half is worshipped in Kalimath. Legend states that the Goddess's face changes from a girl in the morning, to a woman in the afternoon, and an old lady in the evening. She is revered as the protector of the Char Dham. Local belief holds that the removal of her original idol for a dam project in 2013 led to the catastrophic Kedarnath floods, emphasizing her role as the spiritual guardian of the mountains.

How to Travel There

Located near the village of Kalyasaur, it is easily accessible from the main highway:

  • Base: Srinagar (Garhwal), located about 15 km from the temple.
  • Road: The temple is directly on the Rishikesh-Badrinath National Highway (NH-58).
  • Rail: The nearest railway stations are at Rishikesh and Haridwar.
  • Air: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun, is approximately 140 km away.

Quick Facts

Deity: Goddess Kali (Shakti)

Best Time: All year round (October to March is ideal)

Significance: Guardian of Uttarakhand

Winter Places Nearby

While visiting Dhari Devi during the winter season, explore these nearby gems:

  • Srinagar Town: The historical capital of the Garhwal Kingdom.
  • Khirsu: A quiet hill station 30 km away known for oak and deodar forests.
  • Pauri: Offers panoramic views of the snow-capped Himalayan peaks.
